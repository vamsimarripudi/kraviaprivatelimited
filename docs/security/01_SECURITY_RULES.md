# Security Rules

## Purpose

These rules protect KRAVIA company information, private documents, financial records, legal records, and user accounts.

## Account Rules

- Use your own account only.
- Do not share passwords.
- Do not ask another user to log in for you.
- Use a strong password.
- Change your password if you suspect exposure.
- Log out on shared or borrowed devices.

## Role Rules

Use the lowest access level that works:

- Founder for full company control.
- Director for operational work.
- Viewer for read-only review.

Do not give founder access to users who only need to view records.

## Document Rules

- Upload documents only through Document Vault.
- Download documents only when needed.
- Do not share downloaded files without approval.
- Do not expose file paths.
- Do not place company documents in public folders.
- Report missing, incorrect, or exposed documents immediately.

## Data Rules

- Do not enter fake data.
- Do not enter placeholder records.
- Do not use screenshots as official records.
- Do not edit historical records to hide mistakes.
- Use notes to explain corrections clearly.

## Device Rules

- Use trusted devices.
- Keep device lock enabled.
- Avoid public computers.
- Avoid unsecured Wi-Fi for sensitive work.
- Keep browsers and operating systems updated.

## AI Assistant Rules

- Use AI Assistant only for permitted company records.
- Do not paste private secrets into AI prompts.
- Do not treat AI output as legal, tax, or financial advice.
- Verify AI summaries against source records.
- If the system says no information is available, update the source records instead of guessing.

## Incident Rules

Report immediately if:

- A password may be exposed.
- A document was sent to the wrong person.
- An unknown login is noticed.
- A user has too much access.
- Data appears incorrect or missing.
- The system behaves unexpectedly.

## Founder Security Checklist

Review regularly:

- Active users
- User roles
- Audit logs
- Document access
- Failed login patterns
- Backup status
- Open incidents
