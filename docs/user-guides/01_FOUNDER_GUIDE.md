# Founder Guide

## Purpose

This guide explains how a KRAVIA founder should use KRAVIA Company OS to manage company records, decisions, documents, compliance, finance, products, contacts, reports, and audit history.

Founder access is the highest access level. Use it carefully.

## What Founders Can Do

Founders can:

- View all company records.
- Create and edit company information.
- Upload, edit, download, and archive documents.
- Create, edit, and archive meetings, tasks, products, contacts, compliance items, and financial records.
- View audit logs.
- Generate reports.
- Use the Executive AI Assistant where available.

## Daily Start

1. Open KRAVIA Company OS.
2. Log in with your founder account.
3. Review notifications.
4. Check open tasks and overdue items.
5. Check upcoming compliance items.
6. Review recent announcements.
7. Update records only when there is real information to record.

## Company Profile

Use Company Profile as the single source of truth for KRAVIA company details.

Steps:

1. Open Company Profile.
2. Review company name, CIN, PAN, TAN, address, directors, shareholders, and capital details.
3. Select edit only when information has changed.
4. Enter accurate values.
5. Save changes.
6. Confirm the audit log records the update.

Do not enter placeholder values.

## Document Vault

Use Document Vault for important company files.

Steps:

1. Open Documents.
2. Search first to avoid duplicate uploads.
3. Select the correct document category.
4. Upload the file.
5. Add a clear title and description.
6. Confirm the document appears in the list.

Keep all private documents inside the system. Do not share direct file paths.

## Board Meetings

Use Board Meetings for formal company decisions.

Steps:

1. Create a meeting record before or after the meeting.
2. Add meeting title, date, type, and attendees.
3. Add agenda items.
4. Record discussion notes.
5. Record decisions and resolutions.
6. Add action items with owners and due dates.
7. Mark the meeting completed only after required information is entered.

## Financial Records

Use Financial Records for founder-level monthly summaries.

Steps:

1. Open Financial Records.
2. Add one record per reporting month.
3. Enter revenue, expenses, GST collected, and GST paid from real records.
4. Let the system calculate profit/loss and net GST position.
5. Add founder notes only when useful for review.
6. Save the record.

Do not enter estimated numbers unless they are clearly marked in notes.

## Compliance and Tasks

Use Compliance for legal and statutory obligations.

Use Tasks for work that must be assigned and tracked.

Steps:

1. Review overdue and upcoming compliance items.
2. Assign active items to a responsible person.
3. Create tasks for follow-up work.
4. Update statuses as work moves forward.
5. Archive only when the record is no longer active and should be kept for history.

## Products and Contacts

Use Products Portfolio to track KRAVIA products.

Use Contacts & Partners to track important people and organizations.

Steps:

1. Add only real product or contact records.
2. Keep status, risks, milestones, and follow-up dates current.
3. Review risks and follow-ups during weekly review.

## Reports, Search, and AI Assistant

Use Reports for structured review.

Use Search to find existing records.

Use AI Assistant only to summarize stored company information. If information is missing, treat the answer as incomplete and update the source records first.

## Founder Rules

- Keep records accurate.
- Do not upload unnecessary files.
- Do not share login details.
- Do not use personal email for private company documents.
- Review audit logs after major edits.
- Use Viewer access for people who only need to read records.
